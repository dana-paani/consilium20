Consilium’20 is the 6th edition of the Annual Business Conclave of NSUT, organised by the Finance and Economics Society (FES). With a line of eminent speakers and thought provoking events, Consilium has gained the stature of being a platform for young minds to come together and deliberate upon the ever changing corporate world. Consilium has always been one of the biggest and most competitive Business Conclaves in the Delhi Circuit providing business enthusiasts a platform to connect,interact and engage.

The Finance and Economics society of NSUT is a dynamic platform where people discuss and deliberate over business ethics, stock market and burning issues in the finance and economics world. At FES, we believe that it's important that every individual, irrespective of field not only gets acquainted with basics of finance and economics but we aim to nurture an interest for the same.

Ecomanoeuvre: Redefining Economic Policies

EconomicsCPI

“Economic policy that adheres to the tenets of orthodoxy while failing to deliver for large sections of society is doomed to fail.”
Arancha Gonzalez, Spanish Economist

The Finance & Economics Society of NSUT is proud to present Ecomanoeuvre - the flagship economics event of Consilium - The NSUT Business Conclave. Today, we stand at a juncture where old economic policies plague our nation’s economic architecture, acting as a bottleneck to a more liberal state. This is where you step in, as economic policymakers of this country. You have a chance to redefine economic policies, restructure the economic fabric and revamp the economic structure of our country.

Time is flying by and the nation stands at a standstill, you have the power. What will you do?

The competition consists of two rounds:

Round I: This is an online round where the participant will be given a case study. Analyzing through a multidisciplinary approach they would have to deconstruct the problem, proffer policy solutions and contemplate any possible ramifications.
The solutions have to be submitted before 20th January. Registrations will remain open on a rolling basis.

Round II: The shortlisted teams will be called for an on-campus round, details of which will be mailed to the participants.

Rules & Regulations:
The event is open to all undergraduate students.
Participation is allowed in teams comprising of a maximum of 3 members.
Lone Teams and cross-college teams are also permitted.
